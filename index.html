The Chinese Zodiac Clock

This visualization is based on the traditional Chinese sexagenary cycle (Ganzhi 干支), which combines two repeating sequences: 10 Heavenly Stems (element + yin/yang) and 12 Earthly Branches (the zodiac animals). Each year is defined by a unique stem–branch pair, and the complete pattern repeats every 60 years. The outer ring shows the complete cycle, while the inner rings show the elemental and zodiac animal cycles.

Year boundaries:
Chinese New Year follows the traditional lunisolar calendar and is commonly used in popular zodiac traditions.
Lìchūn (立春), “Start of Spring”, is one of the 24 Solar Terms (二十四节气) and marks the beginning of the solar year used in many BaZi systems. It is an astronomical moment, defined by the Sun’s position, and does not fall on a fixed calendar date.
